Understanding Allergies

What are Allergies?

Allergies are immune responses to substances (allergens) that are typically harmless. When a person with allergies comes into contact with an allergen, their immune system reacts excessively, leading to symptoms that can range from mild to severe.

Common Causes of Allergies

Allergies can be triggered by a variety of substances, including:

  • Pollen (from trees, grasses, and weeds)
  • Dust mites
  • Mold spores
  • Animal dander (skin flakes, urine, saliva from pets)
  • Food (such as nuts, dairy, shellfish)
  • Insect stings (like bees or wasps)
  • Certain medications

How to Prevent Allergies

While it may not be possible to completely avoid allergens, you can take steps to minimize your exposure:

  • Avoid known allergens.
  • Keep windows closed during high pollen seasons.
  • Use air purifiers with HEPA filters.
  • Regularly clean your living space to reduce dust and mold.
  • Wash bedding in hot water weekly.
  • Keep pets out of your bedroom.

Treatment and Management of Allergies

Treating allergies typically involves:

  • Avoidance: The most effective way to manage allergies is to avoid the triggers.
  • Medication: Over-the-counter or prescription antihistamines, decongestants, and nasal corticosteroids can alleviate symptoms.
  • Immunotherapy: Allergy shots or oral tablets can help desensitize the immune system to specific allergens.
  • Emergency Treatment: For severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), carry an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) and know how to use it.

When to See a Doctor

If you experience severe allergic reactions or if your symptoms interfere with your daily life, consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice and treatment options.
